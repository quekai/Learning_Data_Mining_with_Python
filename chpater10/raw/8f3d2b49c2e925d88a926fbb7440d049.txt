(Photo: Georg Wendt/AFP/Getty Images)</p></div></div></div><div class="field field--name-body field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden"><div class="field__items"><div class="field__item even"><div itemprop="articleBody"><meta itemprop="image" content="https://www.commondreams.org/sites/default/files/headline/thumbs/eib-draft-proposal-phase-out-fossil-fuel.jpg"><meta itemprop="wordCount" content="577"><p>Climate campaigners cheered on Friday after the European Investment Bank revealed a proposal to phase out funding for fossil fuel projects by the end of 2020.</p>

<p>In a nod to activists pushing for a "fossil-free EIB," the financing institution, which is the lending arm of the E.U., said the climate-friendly plan came after listening "to NGOs, public institutions, and a variety of stakeholders to review our energy lending policy."</p>

<p>"If that doesn't give you that happy #FridayFeeling we don't know what will," <a href="https://twitter.com/FoodWaterEurope/status/1154770472816582656">tweeted</a> the European arm of Food &amp; Watch Watch.</p>

<p>Other groups took to Twitter the celebrate the development as well:</p>

<div align="center"><blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><p>BREAKING: EU's public bank <a href="https://twitter.com/EIB?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">@EIB</a> plans to go <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/fossilfree?src=hash&amp;ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">#fossilfree</a>! <br /><br />A crack of light in the darkness â the EU &amp; national govts are floundering as the planet burns, but the <a href="https://twitter.com/EIB?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">@EIB</a> has made the brave, correct and just proposal to stop funding fossil fuel projects<a href="https://t.co/qKM9ZcvTsJ">https://t.co/qKM9ZcvTsJ</a></p>â Friends of the Earth (@foeeurope) <a href="https://twitter.com/foeeurope/status/1154740251971268608?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">July 26, 2019</a></blockquote><blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><p>Huge news! <p>In a draft <a href="https://www.eib.org/attachments/draft-energy-lending-policy-26-07-19-en.pdf">paper</a> (pdf) on its new energy lending policy dated July 24 and released on Friday, the EIB says:</p>

<blockquote><p>the bank will phase out support to energy projects reliant on fossil fuels: oil and gas production, infrastructure primarily dedicated to natural gas, power generation or heat based on fossil fuels. These types of projects will not be presented for approval to the EIB Board beyond the end of 2020. While it fully understands the role fossil fuels will continue to play within E.U. energy systems for at least the coming decade, the Bank provides higher additionality by focusing on the longer-term challenge and investment needs of the energy sector. As a result, all the Bankâs activities in the energy sector will be fully aligned with the Paris Agreement.</p></blockquote>

<p>The EIB also shared video of bank vice president Andrew McDowell summing up the new proposals, which he said would be in line with the "need to support the energy infrastructure of the future."</p>

<div align="center"><blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><p>Over the last few months, we've listened to NGOs, public institutions and a variety of stakeholders to review our energy lending policy. <br />Hereâs our draft proposal for more ambitious <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/ClimateAction?src=hash&amp;ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">#ClimateAction</a> to support the energy transformation <a href="https://t.co/HLxDMb2vJ2">https://t.co/HLxDMb2vJ2</a> <a href="https://t.co/JCuMnFEer5">pic.twitter.com/JCuMnFEer5</a></p>â European Investment Bank (@EIB) <a href="https://twitter.com/EIB/status/1154762026725629953?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">July 26, 2019</a></blockquote></div>

<p>As McDowell said in the video, the proposal still needs the nod of approval of the EIB's board of directors, which is comprised of E.U. finance ministers. They are set to discuss the proposal in September.</p>

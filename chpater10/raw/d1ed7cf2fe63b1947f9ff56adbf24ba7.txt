											<p><span>The fatal stabbing of Russian LGBT activist Yelena Grigoriyeva in St. Petersburg has drawn strong reactions worldwide, with fellow activists lamenting a system that failed to protect her and anti-LGBT figures saying her killing was justified.</span></p>																					</div>
																			<div class="article__block article__block--article article__block--column ">
													<aside class="article__related-article">
		<h6 class="label related-article__label label--default">news</h6>
		<a class="related-article__inner" href="https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2019/07/30/russian-lgbt-activist-killed-after-being-listed-on-saw-inspired-gay-hunting-site" title="Russian LGBT Activist Killed After Being Listed on ‘Saw’-Inspired Gay Hunting Site">
			<h3 class="related-article__title">
				Russian LGBT Activist Killed After Being Listed on ‘Saw’-Inspired Gay Hunting Site			</h3>
			<span class="related-article__cta">Read more</span>
		</a>
	</aside>



																							   												   																					</div>
																			<div class="article__block article__block--html article__block--column ">
											 <p><span>Activists noted that a website that encourages visitors to &ldquo;hunt down&rdquo; people believed to be sexual minorities had <a href="https://vk.com/wall-497578_87811" title="added">added</a>&nbsp;</span><span>Grigoriyeva to a&nbsp;hit list in the weeks before her killing. The website, which took inspiration from the &ldquo;Saw&rdquo; horror film franchise, was </span><a href="https://www.nydailynews.com/news/world/ny-russia-chechnya-comeback-lgbtq-hunt-game-torture-20190718-nts5imix7rhqvioztxehnwaesu-story.html"><span>blocked</span></a><span> in Russia last week.&nbsp;</span></p>
<h3><span>Here&rsquo;s a closer look at what happened and how Russia has reacted to the news:</span><span></span></h3>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>What happened?</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><span>Grigoriyeva, 41, was found with eight stab wounds and signs of strangulation near her home&nbsp;in central St. Petersburg over the weekend.</span></li>
<li><span>Grigoriyeva had </span><a href="https://www.facebook.com/permalink.php?story_fbid=368034237445194&amp;id=100027158615008&amp;__tn__=-R"><span>posted</span></a><span>&nbsp;on her Facebook page about the threats two days before she was found dead.</span></li>
<li><span>On Thursday, Russia's Investigative Committee said it had<a href="https://sledcom.ru/news/item/1376526/"> detained</a> a 38-year-old native of Kyrgyzstan as the main suspect in Grigoriyeva's killing. Investigators said they had evidence to prove that her death was the result of a domestic dispute and that she had been familiar with the suspect.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What were the reactions?</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><span>The Russian anti-LGBT activist Timur Bulatov </span><a href="https://www.znak.com/2019-07-23/peterburgskiy_borec_s_geyami_nazval_ubiystvo_lgbt_aktivistki_nravstvennym_dzhihadom"><span>declared</span></a><span> Grigoriyeva&rsquo;s slaying to be an act of &ldquo;moral jihad.&rdquo; Bulatov told the Znak.com news website that &ldquo;this creature has been punished by the almighty&rdquo; and said he &ldquo;would have handled her on [his] own if the police were unable to.&rdquo;&nbsp;</span></li>
<li><span>Seven LGBT activists listed on the blocked gay-hunting website have </span><a href="https://www.fontanka.ru/2019/07/24/018/"><span>called on</span></a><span> Russia&rsquo;s Investigative Committee to examine whether the website played a role in Grigoriyeva&rsquo;s killing.&nbsp;</span></li>
<li><span>Supporters gathered in St. Petersburg on Tuesday evening to </span><a href="https://www.facebook.com/DinarSpbRu/posts/1426575217493686"><span>hold</span></a><span> single-person pickets in honor of Grigoriyeva&rsquo;s memory.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What are activists saying?</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><span>Fellow activists claim Grigoriyeva had regularly received death threats and reported them to the police, who did nothing to protect her before she was murdered.</span><span></span></li>
<li><span>She &ldquo;encountered a system unprepared, unable and unwilling to defend her,&rdquo; </span><a href="https://www.svoboda.org/a/30071714.html"><span>said</span></a><span> rights campaigner and Grigoriyeva&rsquo;s friend Dinar Idrisov.</span></li>
<li><span>&ldquo;This could be hatred on grounds of political convictions, homophobia or a domestic dispute. Every lead needs to be considered, and those guilty found and punished,&rdquo; said Maxim Olenichev, a lawyer for the Vykhod LGBT rights group.</span></li>
<li><span>&ldquo;Yelena was killed because she was not afraid to tell the truth about the subjects that are traditionally silent in Russia and on the country&rsquo;s state television channels,&rdquo; demonstrator Marina Ken </span><a href="https://apnews.com/742c2630fc224c208a4be540ad9154d7"><span>told</span></a><span> The Associated Press.</span></li>
<li><span>&ldquo;She never concealed her orientation, and she was told she&rsquo;d get killed for that,&rdquo; </span><a href="https://paperpaper.ru/chto-izvestno-ob-ubijstve-peterburgsk/"><span>recalled</span></a><span> Alexei Sergeyev, an activist with the Alliance of Heterosexuals and LGBT for Equal Rights.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What are the authorities saying?</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><span>The St. Petersburg branch of Russia&rsquo;s Investigative Committee has </span><a href="https://spb.sledcom.ru/Novosti/item/1375181/"><span>opened</span></a><span> a criminal case into murder.</span></li>
<li><span>Grigoriyeva&rsquo;s death is being treated as the result of a domestic dispute and not a hate crime, an unnamed source familiar with the investigation </span><a href="http://www.rosbalt.ru/piter/2019/07/23/1793599.html"><span>told</span></a><span> the Rosbalt.ru news website.</span></li>
<li>Police said they had examined &ldquo;multiple&rdquo; complaints from Grigoriyeva, but <a href="https://tass.ru/proisshestviya/6691205">maintained</a> that they all concerned domestic disputes &ldquo;and none of them concerned possible threats to the life of the victim.&rdquo;</li>
</ul>
<p><em>This story was updated on July 25, 2019, to add details about the detained suspect from the Investigative Committee's statement.</em></p> 																					</div>

					<p>A prominent member of Britain&#8217;s Labour party has apologized for spreading anti-Semitism online, four years after posting a video on Facebook that decried a purported &#8220;Israel-British-Swiss-Rothschilds crime syndicate.&#8221;
<p>Shadow immigration minister Afzal Khan apologized during an <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-england-manchester-49130905">interview</a> with the BBC late last week for his 2015 post, which featured American Jewish comedian Jon Stewart and had been captioned with text referring to &#8220;mass murdering Rothschilds Israeli mafia criminal liars.&#8221;

<p>In 2008 Khan received a high national honor, the CBE, for his community and interfaith work, and is co-founder of the Muslim Jewish Forum of Greater Manchester.

<p>&#8220;I am mortified and sincerely sorry about this genuine accident,&#8221; Khan told the British broadcaster. &#8220;I didn&#8217;t read the text below, which contained an anti-Semitic conspiracy about the Rothschilds. I would never have shared it if I had seen that.&#8221;</p>

	<div id="attachment_1884493"  class="wp-caption  alignleft"><img class="size-medium wp-image-1884493" src="https://static.timesofisrael.com/www/uploads/2018/07/000_13T5OI-400x250.jpg" alt="" width="400" height="250" />
		<div class="wp-caption-text">Illustrative: People hold up placards and Union flags as they gather for a demonstration organized by the Campaign Against Anti-Semitism outside the head office of the British opposition Labour Party in central London on April 8, 2018. (AFP/Tolga Akmen)</div>
	</div>
<p>The Labour Against Anti-Semitism movement has called for Khan&#8217;s expulsion from the party.</p>
<p>This is not the first time that Khan&#8217;s rhetoric has come to the attention of British Jews. In 2016, Jewish groups and lawmakers called on the Labour Party to suspend him after it was revealed that he had <a href="https://www.timesofisrael.com/jewish-groups-to-labour-suspend-mep-who-compared-israel-to-nazis/">compared Israel to the Nazis</a>.</p>
<p>âThe Israeli government are acting like Naziâs [sic] in Gaza,&#8221; Kahn tweeted in August 2014, during Israel&#8217;s war with Hamas in Gaza. He later told the<a href="https://www.thejc.com/news/uk-news/labour-shadow-immigration-minister-shared-antisemitic-conspiracy-theory-1.486862"> Jewish Chronicle</a> that his opponents were using the incident in order to &#8220;smear&#8221; him and that he had deleted the tweet.</p>
<p>Last week, a <a href="https://www.timesofisrael.com/poll-corbyn-loses-ground-as-labour-members-see-anti-semitism-as-genuine-issue/">poll</a> conducted on behalf of the Times of London found that support for Labour head Jeremy Corbyn has fallen significantly among party members, partly in response to the anti-Semitism crisis that has developed under his leadership.</p>
<p>Two-fifths of Labour members want Corbyn, who has led the party since 2015, to resign before the next general election while fully a quarter demand that he step down immediately.</p>

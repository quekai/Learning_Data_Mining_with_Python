                            <h2 class="page-header">Iran, Russia to stage joint drills in Persian Gulf: Iran Navy chief</h2>
                            <div class="item-meta">
                                <div class="item-date" id="header-pubdate" data-id="602186">
                                    Mon Jul 29, 2019 05:00PM 
                                     [Updated: Mon Jul 29, 2019 05:11PM ]

                                    <img id="imgPlayback" src="https://cdn.presstv.com/photo/20190729/20a4ec63-788f-4983-b038-76ef0f388b89.jpg" alt="This handout photo made available by the Iranian Navy on February 23, 2019, shows Iranian Navy submarines sailing during a naval drill in the Sea of Oman.                                                                                                 <div class="item-text">
                                    <p><strong>Iranian Navy Commander Rear Admiral Hossein Khanzadi says Iran and Russia have agreed to conduct joint naval drills in the Persian Gulf region &ldquo;in the near future.&rdquo;</strong></p>

<p>Khanzadi, who was attending the Russian Navy Day celebrations in Saint Petersburg, Russia, made the announcement in an interview with the Islamic Republic News Agency on Monday, saying the two sides have&nbsp;reached an agreement on holding the drills in the Indian Ocean, Makran waters, Strait of Hormuz and the Persian Gulf by the end of year.</p>

<p>Heading a military delegation, Khanzadi participated in the Russian Navy Day celebrations, attended by representatives of 20 countries in Saint Petersburg on Sunday.</p>

<p>Elaborating on the outcome of his trip to Russia, Khanzadi said an agreement was signed with the Ministry of Defense of the Russian Federation on behalf of the General Staff of the Armed Forces of the Islamic Republic of Iran.</p>

<p>The Iranian Navy chief further noted that the agreement was&nbsp;the first of its kind between Tehran and Moscow and aimed to boost military cooperation between the two countries particularly with regard to naval forces.</p>

<p>Back in late April, Khanzadi unveiled plans for a joint naval drill with Russia in coming months.</p>

<div class="title"><a href="https://www.presstv.com/Detail/2019/04/29/594646/Iran-Russia-Persian-Gulf-drills-Admiral-Khanzadi-Damavand-destroyer" target="_blank">PressTV-Iran, Russia navies to stage joint drill in Persian Gulf</a></div>
</div>
<a href="https://www.presstv.com/Detail/2019/04/29/594646/Iran-Russia-Persian-Gulf-drills-Admiral-Khanzadi-Damavand-destroyer" target="_blank">Iranian and Russian military forces will stage a rare naval drill in the Persian Gulf later this year, the commander of Iran&rsquo;s navy has announced.</a></div>

<p>The announcement comes as tensions have been running high between Tehran and Washington since last year, when President Donald Trump unilaterally withdrew the US from the 2015 multilateral nuclear deal, officially known as the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), and unleashed the &ldquo;toughest&nbsp;ever&rdquo; sanctions against&nbsp;the Islamic Republic.</p>

<p>Recently, the US has taken a quasi-warlike posture against Iran&nbsp;and stepped up its provocative military moves&nbsp;in the Middle East, among them the June 20 incursion&nbsp;of an American spy drone into the Iranian&nbsp;borders.</p>

<p>The US says it is deploying an aircraft carrier strike group and a bomber task force to the Middle East in a &ldquo;clear and unmistakable&rdquo; message to Iran.</p>

<p>In May, Washington dispatched an aircraft carrier strike group, a bomber task force, and an amphibious assault ship to the Persian Gulf, citing an alleged Iranian threat.</p>

<p>The military deployment&nbsp;sparked global concerns that the US may be planning to wage war on Iran.</p>

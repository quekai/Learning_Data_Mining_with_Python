																											<div class="article__block article__block--html article__block--column ">
											<p><span>Russia plans to educate youngsters in military-patriotic themes in a new $63 million facility at its sprawling military-themed park outside Moscow, the RBC news website reported Monday.</span></p>
<p><span>The militarization of young Russians has been a controversial topic in the past several years, with President Vladimir Putin&rsquo;s </span><a href="https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2019/04/17/russias-fast-growing-youth-army-aimst-to-breed-loyalty-to-the-fatherland-a65256"><span>establishment</span></a><span> of the Youth Army in late 2015 drawing comparisons to the Hitler Youth. With around half a million members aged 8-18, the Youth Army hopes to reach 1 million members by 2020.</span></p>																					</div>
																			<div class="article__block article__block--article article__block--column ">
													<aside class="article__related-article">

			<h3 class="related-article__title">
				Russia’s Fast-Growing ‘Youth Army’ Aims to Breed Loyalty to the Fatherland			</h3>

																							   												   																					</div>
																			<div class="article__block article__block--html article__block--column ">
											 <p><span>High schoolers will once a year gather for a weeklong &ldquo;military-patriotic education&rdquo; camp at the Russian Defense Ministry&rsquo;s $350 million </span><a href="https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2015/06/04/russian-defense-ministrys-patriot-park-to-cost-363-million-a47129"><span>Patriot Park</span></a><span> west of Moscow, RBC <a href="https://www.rbc.ru/society/29/07/2019/5d3707e69a79471291e0f734" title="cited">cited</a> two ministry officials as </span><span>saying</span><span>.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span>&ldquo;We&rsquo;re discussing if [attendance at the youth education center] will be part of a compulsory or voluntary program,&rdquo; one of the sources was quoted as saying.</span></p> 																					</div>
																			<div class="article__block article__block--article article__block--column ">

											 <p><span>The Moscow city and Moscow region administrations will each contribute 2 billion rubles ($31.5 million) to the project, according to one of the sources.</span></p>
<p><span>The &ldquo;Avangard&rdquo; teaching center&rsquo;s construction is expected to begin in September.</span></p>
<p><span>The latest move comes months after state television </span><a href="https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2019/04/10/russia-launches-world-war-ii-themed-tv-channel-targeting-youth-a65175"><span>launched</span></a><span> a new channel targeting Russia&rsquo;s youth with 24/7 programming dedicated entirely to World War II. In March, security officials </span><a href="https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2019/03/13/russia-to-re-educate-brainwashed-youth-in-patriotic-camps-a64787"><span>announced</span></a><span> that delinquent youngsters would be sent to military-patriotic reeducation camps.</span></p> 																					</div>

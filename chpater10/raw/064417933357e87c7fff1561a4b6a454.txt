<p>More than 100 settlements in Irkutsk region are covered with the smoke too. Piuctured: Kirensk, Ust-Ilimsk, Ust-Kut towns.</p>
</div>
<p>Postings from people hit by fires have been dramatic.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&lsquo;Our children are suffocating,&rsquo; one woman posted.</p>
<p>&lsquo;We are right next to the epicentres of wildfires, and we struggle to breathe,&rsquo; complained mother Marina in Evenkia, in the north of Krasnoyarsk region, one of the worst-hit.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Olga from Tura, in the same region, said: &lsquo;We see bears coming out of the taiga one after the other, deer, too, and smaller animals - but mainly bears.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&lsquo;Planes stopped coming because they can&rsquo;t land in thick smog.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&lsquo;People walk around the village in masks but they are no real help as even with a mask on it hurts to breathe.'&nbsp;</p>
<p><iframe width="720" height="593" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/YZYgc2-vM14" frameborder="0"></iframe></p>
<p>More than 1,500 km further west, Valeria Glukhova from the city of Omsk, said: &lsquo;I was at a bus stop and I felt like I was locked inside a gas chamber.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&lsquo;Every time I inhaled the air burnt my lungs. I returned home with awful headache and soon started vomiting.</p>
<p>&lsquo;My husband called an ambulance.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&lsquo;My head is aching, my throat is hurting, my eyes are itchy.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&lsquo;I had to walk for about ten minutes, and now hair smells of smoke so badly, as if I was sitting all night long by a campfire.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&lsquo;I saw many teenagers and children wearing medical masks, but I am pretty sure they are not enough, you literally need a gas mask.&rsquo;</p>
<p><img src="/PICTURES/ECOLOGY/Apocalypse-Siberia-2019/standard_vanavara.jpg" width="720" height="460" alt="Vanavara" title="Vanavara" /></p>

<p><img src="/PICTURES/ECOLOGY/Wildfires-summer-2019/inside_vanavara_5.jpg" width="720" height="534" alt="Vanavara" title="Vanavara" /></p>
<div class="topListAlt">
<p>Vanavara settlement is covered with the smoke, but the head of the village denies any threat to people's health.</p>
</div>
<p>In badly-hit Vanavara locals have posted pictures which have been disputed by local administration chief Alexander Zarubin who accused them in exaggeration, using photoshop or filters, which they deny.&nbsp;</p>
<p>In this settlement a child was shown wearing a mask because of the pollution.</p>
<p>Locals accuse Zarubin of talking &lsquo;nonsense&rsquo;.</p>
<p>A senior regional official denied that the situation was as serious as resdients and the media had claimed. 'This is a common natural phenomenon, it is meaningless to fight with this, and sometimes it can be even harmful. If we have a snowstorm in winter in cold weather, no one comes up with the idea to drown icebergs so that we have a warmer weather.&rsquo; said Krasnoyarsk governor Alexander Uss.</p>
<p><strong>UPDATE</strong>:&nbsp;</p>
<p>Today Russia declared a state of emergency over wildfires in Krasnoyarsk and Irkutsk regions and parts of Sakha (Yakutia) and Buryatia republics.</p>

  <p>Former deputy prime minister Barnaby Joyce has tried to walk back comments he made to a newspaper in which he claimed his $211,000 income was "spread thin".</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Key points:</h2><ul><li>Barnaby Joyce insists he's not "skint" following an interview with News Corp</li><li>He's attracted criticism for saying he's struggling to support his families on $211,000</li><li>Mr Joyce used his personal circumstances to advocate for an increase in Newstart payments</li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Under the headline "I'm skint", the Nationals backbencher used his personal circumstances supporting two families to explain <a href="/news/2019-07-18/barnaby-joyce-joins-calls-to-increase-newstart-allowance/11322992" target="_self" title="">his calls for an increase in Newstart payments for jobseekers</a>.</p><p>The article prompted criticism from his fellow parliamentarians, prompting Mr Joyce to clarify his circumstances.</p><p>"I am not skint, very good headline," he told reporters at Parliament House.</p><p>"You've all read it but I am not skint … I am on an incredibly good wage and even on that there come times where you've really got to budget because of circumstances that I brought on myself," he said.</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"If I am doing that on an incredible wage, how on Earth is someone doing it on 280 bucks a week?"</p></blockquote><p>Mr Joyce supports two families, having separated from his wife, Natalie, the mother of four of his children.</p><p>He has since had two children with his new partner, former staffer Vikki Campion.</p><p>In the article, Mr Joyce said he eats his own sheep, rarely eats out and switches off the heater on cold winter nights.</p><p>Mr Joyce said he had called for an increase to Newstart because he had a new understanding for how tough it would be to live on.</p><p>"It's not that I'm not getting money, it's just that it's spread so thin," he told News Corp's The Courier-Mail.</p><div class="media-wrapper-dl inline-content video full" data-uri="coremedia://video/11323458">

If they are single they live on about $40 a day.</p><p>The payment has not increased in real terms for 25 years.</p><p>Welfare groups, the business community and politicians across the political spectrum have called for an increase in Newstart payments.</p><p>The Government has repeatedly rejected those calls and insisted two-thirds of people on Newstart found work within 12 months.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">

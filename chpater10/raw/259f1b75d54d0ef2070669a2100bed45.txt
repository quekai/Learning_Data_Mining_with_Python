She said his life was not in danger.</p><p>But Navalny took to social media to say he believed Russian officials were stupid enough to try to poison him in prison. </p><h2>Russia's rulers 'stupid enough,' Navalny says</h2><p>"Are they really such idiots to poison you in a place where suspicions point only at them?" Navalny wrote on Facebook. </p><div data-oembed-url="https://www.facebook.com/plugins/post/oembed.json/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.facebook.com%2Fnavalny%2Fposts%2F2607818185903915" class="widget widget--type-facebook widget--size-fullwidth widget--align-center"><div class="widget__wrapper"><figure class="widget__figure"><div class="widget__ratio widget__ratio--auto"><div class="widget__contents"><div id="fb-root"></div><script async="1" defer crossorigin="anonymous" src="https://connect.facebook.net/fr_FR/sdk.js#xfbml=1&amp;version=v4.0"></script><div class="fb-post" data-href="https://www.facebook.com/navalny/posts/2607818185903915" data-width="552"><blockquote cite="https://www.facebook.com/navalny/posts/2607818185903915" class="fb-xfbml-parse-ignore"><p>&#1059; &#1084;&#1077;&#1085;&#1103; &#1085;&#1080;&#1082;&#1086;&#1075;&#1076;&#1072; &#1085;&#1077; &#1073;&#1099;&#1083;&#1086; &#1072;&#1083;&#1083;&#1077;&#1088;&#1075;&#1080;&#1080;. &#1053;&#1080; &#1085;&#1072; &#1077;&#1076;&#1091;, &#1085;&#1080; &#1085;&#1072; &#1087;&#1099;&#1083;&#1100;&#1094;&#1091;, &#1085;&#1080; &#1085;&#1072; &#1095;&#1090;&#1086; &#1076;&#1088;&#1091;&#1075;&#1086;&#1077;. &#1052;&#1086;&#1075;&#1091; &#1083;&#1077;&#1078;&#1072;&#1090;&#1100; &#1074; &#1089;&#1090;&#1086;&#1075;&#1077; &#1089;&#1077;&#1085;&#1072; &#1074; &#1084;&#1072;&#1077; &#1084;&#1077;&#1089;&#1103;&#1094;&#1077; &#1080;...</p>Publi&eacute;e par <a href="https://www.facebook.com/navalny/">&#1040;&#1083;&#1077;&#1082;&#1089;&#1077;&#1081; &#1053;&#1072;&#1074;&#1072;&#1083;&#1100;&#1085;&#1099;&#1081;</a> sur&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/navalny/posts/2607818185903915">Lundi 29 juillet 2019</a></blockquote></div></div></div></figure></div></div><p>"It's a good question. For now, I can say one thing with certainty: the people in power in Russia are really quite stupid guys. It seems to you that in their actions you need to look for secret meaning or a rational purpose. But in fact they are just stupid, malicious and obsessed with money."</p><p>Posting a picture of himself with a puffy face unable to open either eye, he pointed to what he said was the previous poisoning of two other opposition activists and said his own eyes had swollen up to the size of ping pong balls.</p><p>His own doctor and lawyer said they opposed the "strange" decision to return Navalny to a prison cell where they believe he was somehow poisoned in the first place.</p><p>"He was really poisoned by some unknown chemical substance," Olga Mikhailova, his lawyer, told reporters. "But what the substance was has not been established."</p><p>Mikhailova said she would file an appeal to try to get his jail time cut short due to his health problems.</p><p>"We cannot rule out that toxic damage to the skin and mucous membranes by an unknown chemical substance was inflicted with the help of a 'third party'," Anastasia Vasilyeva, his personal doctor, wrote on Facebook.</p><p>Vasilyeva, who said Navalny had a rash on his upper body, skin lesions, and discharge from his eye, called for samples of Navalny's bedsheets, skin and hair to be tested for chemicals.</p><p>She said she found the fact that she had not been allowed to examine him properly suspicious.</p><h2>Police crackdown on opposition</h2><p>Navalny's sudden hospitalisation infuriated his supporters. It comes after police rounded up more than 1,000 people in the Russian capital at a march on Saturday in one of the biggest crackdowns of recent years against the opposition.</p><p>Navalny, Russia's most prominent opposition figure, has served several stints in jail in recent years for organising anti-government demonstrations.</p><p>The European Court of Human Rights last year ruled that Russia's arrests and detention of Navalny in 2012 and 2014 were politically-motivated and breached his human rights, a ruling Moscow called questionable.</p><p>Want more news?</p><div class="widget widget--type-freeform

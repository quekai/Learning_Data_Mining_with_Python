											 <p><span>European scientists have <a href="https://atmosphere.copernicus.eu/cams-monitors-unprecedented-wildfires-arctic">rung the alarm</a> about the 100 and counting &ldquo;unprecedented" wildfires that have hit Russia's Arctic and Siberia since June. An estimated 2 million hectares of land are currently <a href="https://aviales.ru/popup.aspx?news=5485" title="burning">burning</a>, according to Russia's Aerial Forest Protection Service, roughly compareable in size to the territory of Israel.</span></p> 																					</div>
																			<div class="article__block article__block--image article__block--column ">
											<figure class="article__image">
	<img src="https://static.themoscowtimes.com/image/1360/07/4bf40fa8-d44d-4875-bd66-7bfec3283eea_png_880x1000_x-False_q85.jpg" />

					</figcaption>
	</figure>																					</div>
																			<div class="article__block article__block--html article__block--column ">
											 <p><span>The largest fires &mdash; <a href="http://www.mnr.gov.ru/press/news/glava_minprirody_rossii_poruchil_rosleskhozu_skoordinirovat_deystviya_regionalnykh_lesnykh_vedomstv_/" title="thought">thought</a> to be triggered by a combination of lightning and human activity &mdash; have hit the Irkutsk, Krasnoyarsk and Buryatia regions. </span></p>
<p><span>Smog has reached major cities including Novosibirsk and Tomsk, The Siberian Times </span><a href="https://siberiantimes.com/ecology/others/news/major-siberian-cities-choking-from-strong-wildfires/"><span>reported</span></a><span> Monday, leading to a rise in complaints about air pollution.&nbsp;</span><span>President Vladimir Putin&rsquo;s envoy in Siberia&nbsp;</span><a href="https://tass.ru/sibir-news/6696563"><span>said</span></a><span> Thursday that there was no need to declare an emergency unless carbon monoxide concentration exceeds allowable limits.&nbsp;</span></p> 																					</div>

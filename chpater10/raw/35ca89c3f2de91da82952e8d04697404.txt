  <p>Canadian police have released new CCTV footage of Bryer Schmegelsky and Kam McLeod, the two teenagers wanted in connection with three murders, including that of Australian man Lucas Fowler.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2>Key points: </h2><ul><li>In the footage, Bryer Schmegelsky and Kam McLeod are seen casually walking around a store in Meadow Lake, Saskatchewan</li><li>Canadian police are going door-to-door in a search for the two, centred around the town of Gillam in Manitoba</li><li> The bodies of Lucas Fowler, 23, and Chynna Deese, 24, were discovered on a highway in British Columbia on July 15</li></ul></div>
</div><p> </p><p>In the footage released by police on Friday (local time), the two are seen casually walking around a store in Meadow Lake, in the Canadian province of Saskatchewan.</p><p>The Royal Canadian Mounted Police, known colloquially as Mounties, said the footage was shot on July 21, six days after the bodies of Sydney man Mr Fowler, 23, and his American girlfriend Chynna Deese, 24, were discovered on a highway in British Columbia.</p><p>The Mounties are using helicopters and tracker dogs to search for Mr Schmegelsky and Mr McLeod, and have also called in air support from the Canadian military.</p><p>The hunt for the accused murderers has since shifted 1,300 kilometres east to the remote rural town of Gillam in the province of Manitoba.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">
    <a href="/news/2019-07-27/canadian-police-head-towards-a-helicopter-1/11353060">











  
  
  
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <img src="https://www.abc.net.au/news/image/11353054-3x2-700x467.jpg" alt="Canadian police officers walk towards a yellow helicopter on a forest road." title="Canadian police head towards a helicopter" width="700" height="467"/>
</a><a href="/news/2019-07-27/canadian-police-head-towards-a-helicopter-1/11353060" class="inline-caption"><strong>
        Photo:</strong>
       Police have received aircraft support from the Canadian military. <span class="source">(Reuters/RCMP Manitoba)</span>
      </a></div><p> </p><p>The search has been centred on Gillam since the discovery nearby of a burnt-out vehicle in which Mr Schmegelsky and Mr McLeod had been travelling.</p><div class="inline-content right">
    <a href="https://twitter.com/rcmpmb/status/1154851963474980865"><span><strong>External Link:</strong>

canada tweet</span>
        </a></div><p> </p><p>Police are now going door-to-door in the town and say they want to speak to any members of the public who may have unwittingly helped the pair.</p><p>Police said there had been two corroborated sightings of the suspects in the area.</p><p>"It is possible that someone may not have been aware of who they were providing assistance to and may now be hesitant to come forward," </p><p>About 80 tip-offs had been received over the past 48 hours, police said.</p><p>Mr Fowler was the son of NSW Police Chief Inspector Stephen Fowler.</p><p>Mr Schmegelsky, 18, and Mr McLeod, 19, have been charged with second-degree murder over the death of Leonard Dyck, 64, whose body was found two kilometres from the pair's burnt-out ute on July 19.</p><p>The pair are childhood friends from Port Alberni on Vancouver Island.</p><div class="inline-content photo full">

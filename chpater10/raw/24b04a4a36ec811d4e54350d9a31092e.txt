<p class="mol-para-with-font">'This is why we believe change is going to be made.'</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">The hijab debate has further polarised Iranians at a time when the country is buckling under unprecedented US sanctions imposed since the Trump administration pulled out of a 2015 nuclear deal between Iran and world powers last year.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">It is unclear to what extent the government can enforce hijab compliance amid an economic malaise, including a currency collapse and rising housing prices.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">There is anecdotal evidence that more women are pushing back against the dress code, trying to redefine red lines as they test the response of the ruling Shiite Muslim clergy and their security agencies.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">The struggle against compulsory headscarves first made headlines in December 2017 when a woman climbed on a utility box in Tehran's Revolution Street, waving her hijab on a stick.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">More than three dozen protesters have been detained since, including nine who are currently in detention, said Alinejad.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Despite attempts to silence protesters, public debate has intensified, amplified by social media.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Last month, a widely watched online video showed a security agent grab an unveiled teenage girl and violently push her into the back of a police car, prompting widespread criticism.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">President Hassan Rouhani and Iran's supreme leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, have supported a softer attitude towards women who do not comply with the official dress code.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">However, hardliners opposed to such easing have become more influential as the nuclear deal is faltering.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">They have called for harsh punishment, even lashes, arguing that allowing women to show their hair leads to moral decay and the disintegration of families.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">The judiciary recently urged Iranians to inform on women without hijabs by sending photos and videos to designated social media accounts. </p><p></p><p></p>

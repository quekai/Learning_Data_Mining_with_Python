    <div class="article-body">
                    <p>A set of stamps commemorating the first-ever U.S.-North Korea summit in June last year are currently on sale in the North Korean capital, although denuclearization negotiations between the two sides show little sign of moving forward.</p>
<p>The set of three stamps was launched on June 12, the one-year anniversary of the historic summit in Singapore between North Korean leader Kim Jong Un and U.S. President Donald Trump, a female worker at a stamp shop in Pyongyang told Kyodo News on Sunday.</p>
<p><img src="https://img.kyodonews.net/english/public/images/posts/058e78a86dbf49528b5bee24c3143ba4/photo_l.jpg" width="100%" /></p>
<p>The stamps depict the first handshake between Kim and Trump, their singing of a joint agreement following the summit, and the full text of the statement.</p>
<p>Around Tuesday, a month after Kim and Trump met at the inter-Korean truce village of Panmunjeom on June 30 in their third encounter, stamps marking the event are scheduled to be released, the worker said.</p>
<p>South Korean President Moon Jae In, who accompanied Trump to Panmunjeom, will not be appearing in the stamps, the worker added. His absence is a further indication of Pyongyang's eagerness to continue talks with Washington without intervention from Seoul.</p>
<p>North Korea is believed to want the United States to provide security guarantees to Pyongyang. The two countries remain in a technical state of war, as the 1950-1953 Korean War ended in a cease-fire.</p>
<div class="video"><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/I-IC2svFy6o" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></div>
<p>Ensuring the continuation of the current political system led by the Kim family is a long-sought goal by Pyongyang.</p>
<p>On Friday, North Korea's state-run media reported that Kim oversaw the "demonstration" firing of a "new-type tactical guided weapon" on Thursday to send a warning to the South against holding a joint military drill with the United States next month.</p>
<p>North Korea has long called on the United States and the South to halt joint military exercises, which Pyongyang regards as rehearsals for invasion.</p>
<p>In North Korea, stamps commemorating Kim's summits with Chinese President Xi Jinping have been already sold.</p>
